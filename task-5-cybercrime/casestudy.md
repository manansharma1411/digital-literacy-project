## **Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud**

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a rapidly increasing cybercrime in India, where attackers trick users into authorizing fraudulent transactions. This crime mainly targets students, elderly individuals, and people unfamiliar with digital payment systems.

A typical UPI fraud begins when a scammer contacts the victim through a phone call, SMS, or messaging apps like WhatsApp. The attacker pretends to be a bank representative, delivery agent, or even a buyer/seller from platforms like OLX. They create urgency or trust and then send a “Collect Request” or malicious payment link. The victim is misled into believing they will receive money, but instead, they are prompted to enter their UPI PIN, which actually authorizes a payment to the scammer. In some cases, fraudsters also send fake apps or APK files that capture sensitive data.

The consequences can be severe, including immediate financial loss, unauthorized access to bank accounts, and emotional distress. Victims often struggle to recover their money due to delayed reporting or lack of awareness.

This type of fraud highlights the importance of digital literacy. Users must remember that **entering a UPI PIN always means money is being sent, not received**. Avoid sharing OTPs, verify requests carefully, and report suspicious activity immediately to the bank or cybercrime helpline (1930).
